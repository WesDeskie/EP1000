3D printing

So, you have a 3D model, but you have neither the tools nor the craftsmenship to create a physical model. This is where 3D printing is useful. 3D printing allows makers and designers to easily fabricate a design or model with minimal effort, and with the right settings, these prints can look almost perfect.

How does it work?

3D printing falls under a category known as additive manufacturing, and it works by forming the intended shape layer by layer, either by laying molten material on top of each other or otherwise, thus the name "additive".

There are a few types of 3D printing technology, there's "fused deposition modelling" (FDM), "Stereolithography Apparatus" (SLA), or "Selective Laser Sintering" (SLS). These are very different technologies, but the concept remains the same. Forming the model layer by layer.

FDM

In FDM machines, they work by laying down layers of molten plastic on top of each other. It is the most common technology because of the cheaper price of the machines and ease of use.

SLA

In SLA machines, they use a liquid resin, and the layers are polymerized with a UV light. These machines can produce prints with incredible detail.

SLS

In SLS, a laser is used to sinter each layer from a nylon powder. These machines are used to create fuctional mechanical parts.

In this webpage, I will give a brief overview of how to prepare a 3D model for 3D printing on a FDM machine.

Hardware

The machine we will be using is an Ultimaker 2+. It's important to be familliar with the hardware before preparing the model fro printing. So here are some details about the Ultimaker 2+.

Build Volume: 223x223x205mm
Filament diameter: 2.85mm
Nozzle diameter: 0.4mm
Travel speed: 30-300mm/s

This gives us a good idea of what our print settings should be. The machine uses a larger filament diameter which means we can print at a higher speed, around 60mm/s. The nozzle diameter is 0.4, which allows it to print comfortably with 0.2 layer heights. It also lets us know how thick our line width can be. Knowing all this, lets load our model and print settings into our Slicer.

Slicing Models and Slicers

Before we can print our model, we have to prepare it by "Slicing" the model. This works by using a Slicing Software known as a Slicer, to cut our model into many layers, so that our 3D printer can fabricate them. In this webpage I will show you how to use CURA, which you can download here.

Conclusion

3D printing is a useful tool when it comes to prototyping. I was able to fabricate a dimensionally accurate model in 45 minutes without much physical effort, and while my model prints, I have extra time to work on my projects. I hope this is a good example of how 3D printing can help in digital fabrication and prototyping, and I hope you have a better understanding of how to prepare a 3D model for printing.
