Vector Graphics

In this webpage, I will go through what i've learnt about Vector Graphics in this module

What is Vector Graphics?

Vector Graphics is a subset of computer graphics. A Vector image is one that comprises of lines generated by mathematical funtions. This differs largely from Raster Graphics, which are characterized by images comprised of pixels.

Our goal is to create some line art of an animal that we can use as a logo. We can even engrave this onto surfaces using a laser cutter.

Before you get started, here are some things you need.

  • A Vector Editor like InkScape or Adobe Illustrator (I used InkScape for this tutorial)
  • A drawing tablet (Not necessary but it does make things easier)
  • Patience (This process may take a while, especially for beginners)

Lets get started. The steps are as follows:

  • Open up a reference image of your chosen animal.
  • Use the Bezier curve tool to trace out an area of your reference and change the fill to an appropriate colour.
  • Export your canvas as a .png file.
