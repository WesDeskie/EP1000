Raster Graphics

In this webpage, I will go through what i've learnt about Raster Graphics in this module

What is Raster Graphics?

Raster Graphics is a subset of computer graphics. Raster images comprise of pixels on the screen. If you've taken a digital photograph recently, that photo is a good example of a raster image. This differs largely from Vector Graphics, which are characterized by clean lines generated by mathematical functions.

Our goal is to remove the background of a selfie using a Raster Editor such as Photoshop or GIMP, replace it with a seaside image, and resize the image to 1024 pixels. This is a common use of Raster Editors and a useful skill to have.

Before you get started, here are some things you need.

  • A Raster Editor like Photoshop or GIMP (I used Photoshop for this tutorial)
  • A drawing tablet (Not necessary but it does make things easier)
  • Patience (This process may take a while, especially for beginners)

Lets get started. The steps are as follows:

  • Open up your selfie and your background image
  • Select and mask the region you want to cut out
  • Paste your background image into the project and move it behind your selfie.
  • Export your new image as a .png file and resize it to 1024 pixels.
